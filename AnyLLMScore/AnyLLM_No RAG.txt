Answer: Redundant Array of Inexpensive Disks (RAID) is a storage technology that provides improved data reliability and performance by combining multiple physical disks into a single logical unit. RAID techniques include mirroring, where data is duplicated across two or more disks for redundancy; striping, where data is split into smaller pieces and distributed across multiple disks for improved read/write performance; and parity-based RAID, which uses mathematical algorithms to detect and correct errors in the data. By using RAID, organizations can improve the reliability and availability of their data, reducing the risk of security breaches due to data loss or corruption.
